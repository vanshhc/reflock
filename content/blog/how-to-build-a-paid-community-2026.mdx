A paid community is one of the most powerful business models available to creators today. Unlike courses (sold once) or coaching (capped by time), a well-run community generates recurring revenue and compounds as members help each other.

Here's how to build one from scratch.

## Step 1: Pick a focused niche

The biggest mistake new community builders make is going too broad. "Personal development" is not a niche. "UPSC preparation for working professionals" is.

A tight niche means:
- Easier to market
- Members feel they belong
- Content stays relevant
- Word-of-mouth spreads faster

## Step 2: Validate before building

Before you set up any platform, ask 20 people from your audience:

> "I'm thinking about starting a paid community for [niche]. Would you join for ₹X/month? What would make it worth it for you?"

If 5–8 say yes without prompting, you have something. If fewer, tweak the niche or value proposition.

## Step 3: Set your price correctly

Indian community pricing tends to cluster around:

- **₹299–₹499/month** — accessible, high volume, lower commitment
- **₹999–₹1,999/month** — mid-tier, needs a strong value prop
- **₹2,999+/month** — premium, needs direct access to you or exclusive perks

Start at the middle tier. It's easier to keep at ₹999 than to raise from ₹299.

## Step 4: Design the onboarding experience

The first 7 days determine whether a member stays for 7 months. Your onboarding should:

1. Send a welcome message within 1 hour of joining
2. Give them one specific action to take immediately (introduce themselves, complete a worksheet, attend a call)
3. Connect them with at least one other member

## Step 5: Create consistent rhythm

Members don't churn because of price — they churn because they forget the community exists. Establish weekly touchpoints:

- Weekly Q&A or office hours
- Weekly roundup or newsletter
- Monthly challenge or live event

## Step 6: Reduce churn proactively

Watch for members who go silent. A quick DM ("Hey, noticed you haven't joined the last few calls — anything I can help with?") can save a cancellation.

Give annual plan discounts (2 months free) to lock in committed members.

---

## The numbers that work

A community of 100 members at ₹999/month = **₹99,000/month recurring**. With 20 hours of your time.

That's the math that makes paid communities the most scalable product in the creator economy. Start small, stay consistent, and grow from there.
