India has over 500 million internet users, and a growing segment of them are willing to pay for high-quality, focused knowledge. Yet most creators leave money on the table by relying entirely on ad revenue or brand deals.

Here's how to change that.

## Start with what you already know

You don't need to be the world's top expert. You need to know more than the person one step behind you. If you've cracked CAT, built a business in Tier 2, or mastered a niche skill, there's an audience willing to pay for your shortcut.

## Pick the right product format

Different expertise maps to different products:

- **Templates** — for designers, marketers, founders. Sell once, earn repeatedly.
- **Cohort courses** — for skills that need accountability (fitness, coding, writing). Higher price, higher commitment.
- **Paid communities** — for ongoing support, networking, or niche interests. Monthly recurring revenue.
- **1-on-1 coaching** — highest price point, capped by your time. Best as a premium tier.

## Price it like a professional

Indian creators chronically underprice. A well-positioned ₹999 template that solves a real problem will outsell a ₹99 one that looks cheap. Price signals quality.

A good rule of thumb: what is one hour of the buyer's time worth? Your product should save or earn them at least that.

## Distribution matters more than perfection

The biggest mistake: waiting until the product is "ready." Launch with an early-access cohort of 20 people. Collect testimonials. Improve. Relaunch.

Your first 50 buyers will come from your existing audience — Twitter, LinkedIn, YouTube, or a WhatsApp community you're already in.

## Use a platform that doesn't take a huge cut

Platforms like Gumroad charge 10%+. Building a custom Razorpay checkout is technical overhead. The best middle ground: a creator marketplace that handles discovery, payments, and credibility — so you focus on building, not selling.

The knowledge economy in India is still early. The creators who start building products today will own the market in 2027.
